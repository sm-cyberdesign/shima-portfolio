About Me

Cybersecurity through
a human lens.

I see cybersecurity and UX design as two sides of the same problem: both are about understanding human behavior, reducing confusion, and helping people make safer decisions under pressure.

Security often fails when tools are too complicated, warnings are unclear, or users feel forced to work around the system. My focus is on designing security experiences that make the safe choice feel natural, simple, and trustworthy.

I approach cybersecurity through a human-centered lens: how people click, respond, ignore alerts, reuse passwords, trust interfaces, and make decisions when they are busy, stressed, or unsure. This perspective helps me think beyond technical controls and focus on how security actually works in real life.

I also use AI as a practical leverage tool for cybersecurity learning, research, documentation, content design, and workflow acceleration. I work with structured prompting, canvas-style workflows, AI-assisted analysis, and modern tools to turn complex security ideas into clearer, more usable experiences.

Product Vision

AI Cyber Safety Companion

Future SaaS Concept

Human-centered protection for families, kids, older adults, and non-technical users.

SM Cyber Design LLC is exploring an AI-powered cyber safety companion designed to help everyday users make safer digital decisions before they click, reply, download, or share sensitive information.

Inspired by the simplicity of old antivirus tools but redesigned for today’s scam-driven internet, the product vision focuses on phishing awareness, suspicious-link checking, scam message review, safe online behavior, and simple guidance for vulnerable users.

For KidsSafe clicking, stranger-danger awareness, cyberbullying guidance, password habits, and gamified safety lessons.
For Older AdultsScam message checking, fake bank alert detection, suspicious links, and calm step-by-step safety guidance.
For FamiliesOptional trusted-contact alerts, family safety education, and simple explanations without technical jargon.
For CommunitiesCyber safety support for schools, libraries, spouse groups, nonprofits, and senior communities.

“Attackers exploit human friction. When security controls are too difficult to follow, users invent workarounds that open back doors. True security is not about forcing compliance; it is about making the secure path the easiest path.”
